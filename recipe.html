Tastiest Nigerian Moi Moi

About recipe

Soft, fluffy, and savory, Moi Moi is Nigeria’s beloved steamed bean pudding. Blended beans, peppers, and onions create a rich base full of flavor. Hints of spice, oil, and optional fillings like eggs or fish make it extra hearty. A wholesome dish that pairs beautifully with rice, pap, or enjoyed on its own.

Recipe information

Cooking Duration: 1 hr 15 min (15 min prep + 60 min steaming)

Serves: 5 to 6 people

Difficulty Level: Intermidiate

Ingredients

  • 2 cups peeled beans (black-eyed peas or brown beans)
  • 1 large red bell pepper (tatashe)
  • scotch bonnet peppers (adjust to taste)
  • 1 medium onion
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil (or palm oil, or mix)
  • 2 seasoning cubes (Maggi/Knorr)
  • 1 tsp ground crayfish (optional but traditional)
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg (optional, adds depth)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 2 cups warm water or stock (for mixing)
  • Extras (optional): boiled eggs, corned beef, fish, prawns

Instructions

  1. Soak & peel: Soak beans 10–15 min, rub to remove skins, rinse until clean
  2. Blend: Blend beans with red pepper, scotch bonnets & onion until smooth.
  3. Mix: Stir in oil, seasoning cubes, crayfish, nutmeg & salt. Add warm water/stock until light and fluffy.
  4. Fill: Scoop into Moi Moi leaves, ramekins, or foil; add fish, corned beef, or eggs if desired.
  5. Steam: Place in a pot with a little water, cover & steam 45–60 min, topping up with hot water as needed.
  6. Serve: Check with a fork (comes out clean). Enjoy with rice, pap, bread, or on its own.


Crispy Smashed Potato Salad

About Recipe:

Not your average potato salad — this one crackles with crunch. A creamy, tangy dressing adds richness without weighing it down. Fresh veggies bring brightness for a salad that feels both light and hearty. Every bite is bold, balanced, and irresistibly good.

Recipe information

Cooking Duration: 1 hr (20 min boiling + 30 min roasting + 10 min mixing)

Serves: 6 people

Difficulty Level: Beginner

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs baby potatoes
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika (optional)
  • Salt & black pepper
  • ½ cup mayo (or Greek yogurt)
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar or lemon juice
  • 1 tsp honey (optional)
  • 3 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • ½ red onion, diced (or green onion)
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, chopped (optional)
  • 2 tbsp fresh dill or parsley

Instructions

  1. Boil potatoes in salted water until fork-tender (15–20 min).Drain.
  2. Smash on a parchment-lined tray, flatten gently.
  3. Smash on a parchment-lined tray, flatten gently.
  4. Mix dressing: mayo, mustard, vinegar/lemon, honey, salt & pepper.
  5. Combine potatoes, celery, onion, eggs (if using), herbs, and dressing. Toss gently.
  6. Serve warm (crispy) or chilled (flavorful).


Spaghetti Bolognese

About Recipe

A slow-simmered meat sauce wraps spaghetti in rich, savory warmth. Tomatoes, herbs, and tender beef blend into timeless Italian comfort. Each bite is hearty, saucy, and deeply satisfying. A classic dish that feels like home, no matter where you are.

Recipe information

Cooking Duration: 45 min (15 min prep + 30 min simmering)

Serves: 4 Persons

Difficulty Level: Beginner

Ingredients

  • 400 g spaghetti
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 carrot & 1 celery stick, finely diced
  • 500 g ground beef (or beef/pork mix)
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 can (400 g) chopped tomatoes
  • 1 cup beef stock (or water)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano (or Italian seasoning)
  • Salt & pepper
  • Grated Parmesan & fresh basil (to serve)

Instructions

  1. Cook pasta: Boil spaghetti in salted water until al dente. Drain, reserve some pasta water.
  2. Sauté: Heat oil, cook onion, garlic, carrot & celery until soft.
  3. Brown meat: Add ground beef, cook until no longer pink.
  4. Simmer: Stir in tomato paste, tomatoes, stock, oregano, salt & pepper. Simmer 20–30 min until rich.
  5. Combine: Toss spaghetti with sauce (add pasta water if needed).
  6. Serve: Top with Parmesan & basil.


Lumpy Nigerian Egusi Soup

About recipe

Rich, hearty, and deeply flavorful, Egusi Soup is a Nigerian classic made with grounded melon seeds. The lumpy version forms soft, spongy chunks of egusi that soak up the rich stew. Cooked with assorted meats, fish, and leafy greens, it’s comforting, wholesome, and pairs beautifully with pounded yam, eba, fufu, or rice.

Recipe information

Cooking Duration: 1 hr (20 min prep + 40 min cooking)

Serves: 6 to 8 people

Difficulty Level: Advanced

Ingredients

  • 2 cups ground egusi (melon seeds)
  • medium onions (1 blended with egusi, 1 for stock)
  • ½ cup palm oil
  • 3 cups assorted meats (goat, beef, tripe, pomo)
  • 1 cup stockfish/dry fish (pre-soaked)
  • 2–3 cups meat stock (from boiling meat)
  • 2–3 tbsp ground crayfish
  • 2 red bell peppers + 2 scotch bonnets (blended)
  • 2 seasoning cubes (Maggi/Knorr)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • A handful of ugu leaves or spinach (washed & chopped)

Instructions

  1. Prepare proteins: Boil meats with onion, seasoning cubes, and salt until tender. Add stockfish and set aside with stock.
  2. Blend: Blend egusi with half an onion and little water into a thick paste. Blend peppers separately.
  3. Cook sauce: Heat palm oil, add blended peppers, and fry until oil begins to separate. Add meat stock and crayfish.
  4. Form lumps: Drop egusi paste into the pot in small scoops. Let it cook undisturbed for 5–7 min so lumps form, then stir gently.
  5. Combine: Add cooked meats, fish, and pomo. Simmer 10–15 min, adjusting with stock if too thick.
  6. Finish: Stir in vegetables, cook 2–3 min until vibrant green.
  7. Serve: Enjoy hot with fufu, pounded yam, semo, or rice.
